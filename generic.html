My Summer Internship
at the Aquarium

For this internship my partner and I developed an end to end computer vision algorithm
to find similar manatees to the input one.
We also developed a web app for the researchers to use it by uploading an image and scrolling through the matches.

At the Mote Marine Research Lab they keep track of manatees in the area, the way they identify them is by their scars. They have an image database of every manatee they work with but it is hard to keep track of all these images so they needed a way to find a specific manatee given an input image.
The algorithm we developed consisted of focusing on an area specified by the user, in other words, an ROI: region of interest. Given this ROI the algorithm looks at all images in the database and compares the scar's shape against the scar (if any) in every other image. It computes a similarity score we developed and returns the best matches with their name for the user to evaluate.
This project replaced the manual work of scrolling through thousands of images and saved them 30-40hrs a week, increasing productivity by 80%.
